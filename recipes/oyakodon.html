Oyakodon

Description

Oyakodon is a traditional Japanese dish which consists of sauteed chicken cooked in Japanese broth. It is served along with egg and rice.

The recipe below can yield up to 4 servings and can be prepared in 40 minutes.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of uncooked jasmine rice
  • 4 cups water
  • 4 skinless, boneless chicken thighs, chopped into small pieces
  • Half of an onion, sliced into pieces
  • 2 cups dashi stock
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoons mirin (Japanese rice wine)
  • 3 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 4 eggs

Steps

  1. Rinse the rice in 3 to 4 changes of water, or until the water is almost clear.
  2. Place the rice and 4 cups of water and boil in a saucepan over high heat.
  3. After around 20 to 25 minutes, reduce the heat to medium-low. Cover the pan and simmer until the rice is tender and the liquid has been absorbed.
  4. Place the chicken in a nonstick skillet with a lid, and stir over medium heat until it begins to brown. This takes around 5 minutes.
  5. Add the onion and stir until the onion is soft.
  6. Pour in the stock, soy sauce, mirin, and brown sugar. Stir the mixture to dissolve the sugar.
  7. Bring the mixture to a boil and let simmer till slightly reduced.
  8. Whisk the eggs in a bowl until they are well-beaten. After this, pour it over the chicken and stock. Cover the skillet, reduce the heat, and let it steam fir about 5 minutes, or until the egg is cooked.
  9. Place 1 cup of cooked rice per bowl into 4 deep soup bowls, top each bowl with 1/4 of the chicken and egg mixture, and spoon about 1/2 cup of soup into each bowl.
